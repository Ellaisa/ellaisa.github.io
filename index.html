Mike Glennon and Mitch Trubisky show flashes at first Bears practice

Photos from the Bears' first training camp practice in Bourbonnais on July 27, 2017.

Rich CampbellContact Reporter

Camp rundown

Quarterback report

Mike Glennon: He made a strong, accurate throw on the run to connect with Markus Wheaton near the sideline during team drills. Glennon has functional mobility for being 6-foot-6, and when he was playing for the Buccaneers it was not uncommon for him to complete passes on the move. His length is more of a challenge, though, in resetting his throwing motion. If he's loaded up to throw one direction and has to recollect himself and throw elsewhere, it can be difficult corralling his long limbs. That was a focus for him during Bears practices in the spring and will continue in training camp.

Mitch Trubisky: Rookie pleased the crowd during seven-on-seven drills when he completed a long ball down the right sideline to undrafted rookie Tanner Gentry, who had a step on veteran reserve cornerback Sherrick McManis. Trubisky put just the right amount of air under the ball and Gentry ran under it about 40 yards downfield. It prompted the loudest cheer of the session. Later, Trubisky tried to squeeze a throw over the middle to tight end Daniel Brown through the tightest of windows. Undrafted rookie linebacker Alex Scearce was on Brown's inside hip, though, and tipped the ball away.

Seen and heard

Bears Chairman George McCaskey rolled up to practice Wednesday morning on a scooter, the type that requires good old-fashioned foot power to operate. While several Bears coaches and players get around campus on bicycles, McCaskey apparently prefers a smaller mode of transportation. A couple of hours after practice ended, McCaskey was seen again pedaling around.

Another comeback

Fans greeted receiver Kevin White enthusiastically as he jogged out to practice wearing his new jersey number (He has switched from 13 to 11, which he wore at West Virginia.) Fans hadn't seen White in a Bears helmet since Oct. 2, when an awkward tackle resulted in torn ligaments in his left ankle and a spiral fracture in his fibula. He returned to practice in the spring and was fully fit, but Wednesday was a public unveiling of sorts. As a rookie in 2015, he missed all of training camp with a stress fracture in his left tibia, which sidelined him for the whole season. Since White was drafted seventh overall, he has missed 28 of the team's 32 games. "When you get something taken away from you," he said, "you enjoy it a little more."

Injury report

•S Deon Bush (dehydration) did not practice.

•OLB Lamarr Houston (left knee) was limited.
